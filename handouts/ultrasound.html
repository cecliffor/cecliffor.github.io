What is Diagnostic Ultrasound?

Diagnostic ultrasound is a painless, radiation-free imaging technology that uses high-frequency sound waves to create real-time images of the soft tissues inside your foot and ankle. It allows Dr. Clifford to see tendons, ligaments, muscles, nerves, and fluid-filled structures (like cysts or bursa).

Unlike X-ray or MRI, ultrasound shows your tissues moving in real time — so Dr. Clifford can see exactly what happens when you move or press on a painful area.

How Does It Work?

A small handheld device called a transducer is placed on your skin over the area of concern. The transducer sends high-frequency sound waves into your body. These sound waves bounce off your tissues and return to the transducer, which converts them into live images on a screen.

Key Advantages

  • No radiation – Completely safe, no exposure to ionizing radiation
  • Real-time imaging – See tissues moving as you move your foot
  • Quick and painless – Exam typically takes less than 15 minutes
  • No preparation needed – No fasting, no contrast dye, no needles
  • Portable – Can be performed right in the exam room

What Conditions Can Ultrasound Diagnose?

Tendon Conditions

  • Plantar Fasciitis
  • Achilles Tendinitis & Tears
  • Posterior Tibial Tendinitis
  • Peroneal Tendinitis
  • Tendon subluxation or dislocation

Nerve & Soft Tissue

  • Morton's Neuroma
  • Tarsal Tunnel Syndrome
  • Ganglion cysts
  • Bursitis
  • Plantar plate tears

Ligament & Other Conditions

  • Ankle ligament sprains (ATFL, CFL, PTFL)
  • Foreign bodies (splinters, glass, metal)
  • Soft tissue masses or tumors
  • Fluid collections or abscesses

Beyond Diagnosis: Ultrasound-Guided Procedures

In addition to diagnosis, Dr. Clifford uses ultrasound to guide injections and procedures with pinpoint accuracy. This ensures the medication is delivered exactly where it is needed — improving results and reducing side effects.

  • Cortisone injections for arthritis, tendinitis, or neuromas
  • Aspiration (draining) of cysts or fluid collections
  • Regenerative injections (prolotherapy, PRP, homeopathic injectables)
  • Needle guidance for diagnostic aspiration
