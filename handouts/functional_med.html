What is Functional Medicine?

Functional medicine is a holistic approach that looks beyond your symptoms to find the root cause of your foot or ankle pain. Instead of simply treating the pain (with injections, medications, or surgery), functional medicine asks: Why is this happening?

Your feet don't exist in isolation. Chronic inflammation, metabolic issues, nutritional deficiencies, and gut health can all contribute to foot pain, tendinitis, neuropathy, and arthritis. Functional medicine addresses these underlying factors — leading to lasting relief, not just temporary symptom suppression.

How Does It Work?

Dr. Clifford takes a detailed history and may order specialized lab tests to uncover hidden imbalances. Based on the results, he develops a personalized plan that may include:

  • Dietary modifications to reduce inflammation
  • Targeted nutritional supplements (vitamins, minerals, omega-3s, etc.)
  • Lifestyle changes to improve metabolic health
  • Gut health optimization
  • Stress management and sleep improvement

The Inflammation Connection

Chronic, low-grade inflammation is a common driver of foot pain. Poor diet, blood sugar imbalances, stress, and gut issues can keep your body in a constant state of inflammation, making it difficult for injured tendons and ligaments to heal. Functional medicine targets this inflammation at its source.

Common Lab Tests

Depending on your symptoms, Dr. Clifford may recommend testing for:

  • Inflammatory markers – CRP, ESR, ferritin
  • Vitamin and mineral deficiencies – Vitamin D, B12, magnesium, iron, zinc
  • Blood sugar and metabolic health – Fasting insulin, hemoglobin A1c, lipids
  • Autoimmune markers – ANA, rheumatoid factor, anti-CCP
  • Gut health – Food sensitivity testing, microbiome analysis
  • Hormone levels – Thyroid, cortisol

Dietary & Lifestyle Recommendations

Anti-Inflammatory Diet

  • Leafy greens and colorful vegetables
  • Wild-caught fish (omega-3s)
  • Berries and antioxidant-rich fruits
  • Nuts, seeds, and healthy fats
  • Herbs and spices (turmeric, ginger)

Foods to Reduce

  • Refined sugar and processed foods
  • Refined carbohydrates (white flour, white rice)
  • Fried and fast foods
  • Excessive alcohol
  • Highly processed vegetable oils

Lifestyle Factors That Matter

  • Sleep – Poor sleep increases inflammation and impairs healing
  • Stress – Chronic stress raises cortisol, which can worsen pain and inflammation
  • Hydration – Dehydration affects joint lubrication and tissue repair
  • Physical activity – Gentle movement supports circulation and healing

Frequently Asked Questions

Is functional medicine evidence-based?

Yes. The principles of functional medicine are supported by a large body of research on the links between inflammation, nutrition, metabolic health, and chronic disease. Dr. Clifford uses evidence-based testing and interventions.

How long does it take to see results?

Some patients notice improvements in energy and pain levels within a few weeks. However, true healing of chronic inflammation takes time — typically 3-6 months of consistent dietary and lifestyle changes.

Will this replace my other treatments?

Functional medicine complements — rather than replaces — other treatments like orthotics, shockwave therapy, or injections. It addresses the underlying factors that may be preventing you from healing fully.

Do I need special testing?

Not everyone needs extensive testing. Dr. Clifford may recommend specific labs based on your history and symptoms.
