Advanced In-Office Care & Services

Functional Medicine

Foot problems often stem from whole-body inflammation, metabolic issues, or nutritional deficiencies. Functional medicine testing treats the root cause — not just symptoms.

Diagnostic Ultrasound

Painless, radiation-free imaging of tendons, ligaments, and nerves. Ideal for diagnosing plantar fasciitis, tendon tears, neuromas, and cysts.

Custom Orthotics

Prescription devices made specifically for your feet. Correct your unique biomechanics, relieve pressure points, and prevent reinjury.

Shockwave Therapy

Non-invasive sound waves stimulate healing for plantar fasciitis, neuropathy, neuromas, and Achilles tendinitis. No needles, no downtime. Typically 3-5 sessions.

Toenail Removal

Safe in-office removal for infected, ingrown, thickened, or painful nails. Quick procedure that can be made permanent.

Cortisone Injections

Powerful anti-inflammatory shot for arthritis, tendinitis, and plantar fasciitis. Relief begins within days and lasts weeks to months.

Prolotherapy

An irritant solution injected into weakened ligaments or tendons triggers natural healing. Especially effective for ankle instability, plantar fasciitis, and unresolved sprains.

Homeopathic Injection Therapy

A gentle, plant-based alternative to corticosteroids. Reduces inflammation, breaks up scar tissue, and relieves nerve pain without side effects.

Routine Nail and Callus Care

Medical assessment and treatment of thick, fungal, or deformed nails and painful calluses to restore comfort and mobility. Ideal for diabetics and seniors.

Laser Therapy for Pain & Inflammation

Painless light energy reduces inflammation, stimulates cell repair, and improves blood flow. Effective for arthritis, tendonitis, neuropathy, and sports injuries. 3–6 sessions.

Laser Therapy for Toenail Fungus

Specialized laser safely destroys toenail fungus without damaging skin or nail. Painless, no needles, no anesthesia, no downtime.

Minimally Invasive Surgery

Small incisions (less than 1 cm) correct bunions, hammertoes, and neuromas. Less pain, faster recovery, and no large scars.

Focus on conservative care — surgery is always the last resort

Craig E. Clifford, DPM, MHA

Board Certified: American Board of Foot and Ankle Surgery · American Board of Podiatric Medicine

Dr. Clifford completed his residency training at the Franciscan Foot and Ankle Institute in Washington, where he later served as Director of Residency Training. During his teaching tenure, he received multiple honors including Clinician of the Year, Instructor of the Year, and Outstanding Teaching Award. In 2020, he was named Physician of the Year by the Washington State Podiatric Medical Association.

Though highly trained in advanced surgical techniques, Dr. Clifford came to recognize that most foot and ankle surgery can be avoided with appropriate conservative care. In 2022, he left his successful high-volume surgical practice to focus on expanding patient access to cutting-edge nonsurgical treatment—though he continues to perform surgery when necessary, utilizing the latest advancements in minimally invasive techniques. Dr. Clifford understands that there is no "one size fits all" solution in medicine, and every patient deserves an individualized, evidence-based approach, not a generic treatment plan.
